Planning

As RIBA Client Advisers we help clients design the process of delivering projects. Starting from square one, we work with your business plan to map out your objectives. We help appoint the right consultants and set up clear methods of collaborative working. We have a special expertise in participatory design and we use our own toolkit that allows non-professionals to input meaningfully into the design development. We run architecture competitions, write briefs, hold design reviews and have experience in both public and private sector procurement. Each process builds a body of evidence that supports planning applications, delivers the buildings and enables their use.

Projects

RSA Transitions: building a rehabilitation culture

In 2012, the RSA assembled a 3-person team - an Architect, a project manager and a social policy expert - to develop a social enterprise model for the rehabilitation of offenders leaving prison. Working across traditional disciplinary boundaries and together with a pilot prison - HMP Humber - we developed both a template and a site-specific project as a demonstrator. Using a Karthaus-Designed toolkit we engaged around 200 stakeholders at national, regional and local (including offenders inside the prison) to shape and test the model. The RSA Transitions homepage contains the published core document and the masterplan is available here.

Naya Raipur with Les Ateliers

Naya Raipur is a new state capital city for 560,000 people currently under construction in Chhattisgarh, central India. As part of an EU-funded programme of learning between Europe and Asian town planning, Karthaus Design was appointed to lead a professional workshop in the new city together with French Architect Florence Bougnoux. The logistics were organised by Les Ateliers Internationaux, according to their well-established method. 18 multidisciplinary professionals were selected from around 120 international applicants. They worked in teams to develop adjustments to the masterplan, to improve its sensitivity to the rich, existing context and many of the recommendations have now been implemented. The synthesis report can be viewed here.

Cressingham Gardens with Social Life

Cressingham is a unique and well-loved housing estate, overlooking Brockwell Park and designed by Ted Hollamby's team at Lambeth Council in the late '60s and early '70s. There are both design and maintenance problems with some of the homes however and Karthaus Design were appointed to work with Social Life and the residents of the estate to develop new options for its future. We developed a series of massing studies and a development appraisal template that would allow different scenarios to be explored in an open and collaborative manner. We made a 1:250 scale model and hand drawings as collaborative tools to understand how the existing place works through a series of workshops with the residents.

Planning guide for building schools in Lambeth

Karthaus Design are currently commissioned by Lambeth Council to produce a brief guidance document for those making planning applications for schools. The guide addresses some of the common shortfalls in information provided in applications and describes a process that can be followed to ensure applications are sound prior to submission.
